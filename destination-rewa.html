Enchanting Rewa

Blessed with natural beauty, the city of Rewa is situated on the north-western side of Madhya Pradesh. The city has frolicking streams gushing from the mountains and lush green forests.The most visited place in Rewa is a nearby village, Mukundpur,District Satna which is best known for the 'White Tiger Zoo & Safari'. It was home to Mohan - the world renowned white tiger. Watching the surreal white tiger strolling in the wild is the moment you can not miss.

The town is surrounded by dizzyingly high yet beautiful waterfalls like Bahuti fall , Keoti fall, Purva fall and Chachai fall. Bahuti waterfall with a height of 142m, is one of the highest falls of the region.Along with these, the city has kept protected our glorious history in the form of ancient monuments and carvings, like the 35 ft. reclining Bhairav Baba statue at the popular Bhairav Baba Temple. The statue, sculpted out from a single rock, is a must-see place to visit in Rewa.

The Buddhist stupas belonging to Mauryan age at Deur Kothar and marvellous Rewa Fort & museum, Venkat Palace, Govindgarh Palace & lake, and Rani talab make to the list of tourist places to visit in Rewa.

The white tigers, centuries old architecture and the serene nature, The town of Rewa along with Satna promises to offer a rare spectacle; one that gives you cherished moments to remember.

Venkat Bhawan

Venkat Bhawan was built in 1907 by Maharaja Venkatraman Singh of Rewa. Main attraction of the site includes the deity statues, a big fountain and a swimming pool.

Bahuti

Located near Rewa, the Bahuti fall is the majestic and highest waterfall in Madhya Pradesh.

Bhairavnath statue

The 31 feet tall statue of Bhairav Baba is a unique tourist attraction in Rewa.

Chachai

A stunning waterfall that streams from a stunning mountain cliff from a remarkable height. It is said to be the 2nd highest fall of Madhya Pradesh.

Deur Kothar

Buddhist Stupas belonging to Mauryan age

Govindgarh Palace

Wrapped in royalty and nestled in serenity, Govindgarh palace stands strong with elegance.

Kheoti

Kheoti is one of the beautiful waterfalls in the state pouring onto the rugged rocks of mountain.

Mukundpur Zoo

One of its kind, Mukundpur Zoo houses the enigmatic White Tigers.

Purva Waterfall

Water falls into the abyss, creating this thundering yet beautiful waterfall.

Rani talab

Rani talab is one of the oldest water-wells in Rewa, located in the southern part of the city.

Baghela Museum

Baghel Museum exhibits some personal belongings and military weapons once used by the royals of Rewa.
